Holistic Skin Care and Wellness

About this module

Managing eczema well means more than treating flares. What you eat, how you move, how you sleep, and how you handle stress all affect your skin. This module brings those pieces together — with practical strategies you can start using this week, even on your busiest days.

Key points

Read the key points Hide the key points
  • A "good enough" routine beats a perfect routine you can't keep up. Simplify during stressful weeks rather than stopping entirely.
  • Flare prevention starts with knowing your early warning signs and having a plan ready before a flare fully develops.
  • Mindfulness means paying attention to the present moment without judgment. Even five minutes a day can reduce stress and scratch urges.
  • Progressive Muscle Relaxation (PMR) — tensing and releasing muscle groups one at a time — can lower stress and help you sleep.
  • Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) has been shown to reduce itch intensity, sleep problems, anxiety, and depression in people with eczema.
  • A skin-friendly diet is an anti-inflammatory diet: more fruits, vegetables, and whole grains; less processed food and added sugar.
  • No single food causes eczema. Talk to your doctor before cutting out any food group.
  • Regular moderate exercise lowers cortisol, improves sleep, and supports mood.
  • During flares, choose lower-sweat activities and shower with lukewarm water afterward.
  • Alberta-based resources: MindShift CBT app, BounceBack Alberta, AHS Mental Health Helpline (1-877-303-2642), and your family doctor for referrals.

Bring this to your appointment

  • "I'd like help building a 'good enough' routine for stressful weeks."
  • "Could CBT help with my itch and sleep, and how would I access it here?"
  • "I want to add more movement to my week — what works with active eczema?"
  • "Can we talk about how stress and my skin are connected for me specifically?"
