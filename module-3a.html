Building Your Self-Management Skills & Confidence

About this module

Living well with eczema means taking an active role in your own care. That doesn't mean doing it alone — it means knowing your triggers, setting goals that fit your life, recovering from setbacks without giving up, and speaking up for what you need. This module is about building those skills and the confidence that comes with them.

Key points

Read the key points Hide the key points
  • Self-efficacy means believing you can manage your eczema. Believing it makes you more likely to do it.
  • Tracking your symptoms, foods, products, activities, and stress helps you see patterns over time.
  • Apps like EczemaWise (from the National Eczema Association) make tracking easier on your phone.
  • Small consistent habits beat big perfect plans. Start with one thing for one week.
  • "Habit stacking" links a new habit to something you already do — like moisturizing right after brushing your teeth.
  • SMART goals are Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound.
  • Setbacks are part of the journey, not a sign of failure. Treat them as information.
  • The way you talk to yourself matters. "This flare will pass" works better than "this will never get better."
  • An Eczema Action Plan, written with your doctor, lays out your daily care, your early flare signs, your step-up plan, and when to call.

Bring this to your appointment

  • "I'd like to create an Eczema Action Plan with you."
  • "I've been tracking my symptoms — here's what I've noticed."
  • "I have one specific question about my treatment today."
  • "Can we set a goal together for the next few weeks?"
