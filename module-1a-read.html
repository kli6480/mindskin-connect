What is Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis)?

Genetics & Environment

Atopic dermatitis is a common chronic inflammatory skin condition driven by a complex mix of genetics and environmental factors — not by anything you did or didn't do.

Intense Itch

It's characterised by intense, persistent itchiness and skin inflammation that can range from mild to severe.

Any Age

It typically begins in infancy or early childhood, but can also first develop in adulthood — at any stage of life.

Quality of Life

Its effects reach well beyond the skin and can have a profound impact on sleep, relationships, work, and emotional well-being.

The Skin-Mind Link: Introducing Psychodermatology

Your skin and brain are deeply and intricately connected through shared nerve pathways, hormones, and immune signals. Psychodermatology is the field of medicine that studies and addresses this vital link.
Emotional factors like stress, anxiety, and low mood can directly influence skin conditions like eczema — often triggering or significantly worsening symptoms.
And the relationship goes both ways. Living with a visible and sometimes uncomfortable skin condition can, in turn, affect your self-esteem, your confidence, and your mental well-being.

Stress and Your Eczema: A Closer Look

For many adults with eczema, stress is a primary trigger or a major factor in flare-ups. Stressors can be varied: work pressure, relationship difficulties, financial worries, significant life changes, or even the ongoing burden of managing a chronic condition day after day.

The connection isn't always obvious at first. A flare might appear a few days after a stressful event rather than during it. Keeping a simple log of your stress levels alongside your skin symptoms can help you start to see your own patterns.

💡 Recognising your personal stress signals — and noticing how they might connect to your skin — is one of the most empowering steps you can take in managing your eczema.

How Does Stress Make Eczema Worse?

(The Science, Simplified!)

When you experience stress, your brain activates the body's 'fight or flight' response. This triggers a cascade of stress hormones — primarily cortisol and adrenaline — that affect both your immune system and your skin barrier. Think of stress as a factor that can 'turn up the volume' on your eczema symptoms.

The Wider Impact of Eczema on Your Life

Eczema's reach extends beyond your skin. It can affect:

Sleep Quality

Nighttime itch is one of the most common and disruptive symptoms of eczema. Disrupted sleep leads to fatigue, reduced concentration, and lower mood — which can then make eczema harder to manage.

Emotional Well-being

Living with chronic itch and visible skin changes can lead to frustration, embarrassment, anxiety, and low mood. These emotional responses are completely valid and extremely common.

Daily Activities & Social Life

You might find yourself avoiding certain activities, clothing choices, or social situations because of discomfort or concerns about how your skin looks. This can lead to isolation over time.

Work/Study Performance

Discomfort, poor sleep, and emotional distress can all affect your ability to concentrate, be productive, and show up fully at work or in your studies.

Eczema and Your Mental Well-being

It's well-documented that adults with eczema have a significantly higher likelihood of experiencing anxiety and depression compared to people without eczema. This is not a sign of weakness — it is an understandable and recognised consequence of managing a chronic, often unpredictable condition.
💡 If you're struggling with your mood, anxiety, or the emotional weight of eczema, please know that effective support is available — and that bringing it up with your doctor is a completely valid and important part of your care.

Partnering with Your Dermatologist

Your dermatologist can be a key ally. Don't hesitate to:

💬

Discuss the Full ImpactShare how eczema affects your sleep, mood, relationships, work, and daily life — not just the physical symptoms on your skin. Your dermatologist needs the full picture to help you effectively.

🔍

Ask About the Skin-Mind AspectAsk about strategies or resources your care team recommends specifically for managing stress-related flares. This is a legitimate and important part of eczema care.

🤝

Seek ReferralsIf you're struggling emotionally, ask if they can refer you to a mental health professional or support group who is familiar with chronic skin conditions. You don't have to navigate this alone.

📋

Collaborate on Your PlanWork together to find a treatment approach that addresses your skin while genuinely fitting into your real daily life — not just an ideal version of it.
